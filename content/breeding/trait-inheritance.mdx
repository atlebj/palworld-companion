# Breeding Trait Inheritance Explained

Breeding inheritance determines which **passive skills (traits)** a baby Pal can receive from its parents. If you want “perfect” workers or combat Pals, you’re really trying to build a **clean breeding line** where parents have only the passives you want.

## Key idea: fewer random passives = better results
If parents have extra unwanted passives, those can take up trait slots and ruin otherwise good offspring. Most frustration comes from breeding with “dirty” parents.

## What can be inherited
- Passive skills from either parent
- A baby can receive a combination of parent passives (depending on available slots and RNG)

## The reliable method (works in practice)
### Step 1 — Start clean
Use parents that have **only 1–2 desirable passives** each.

### Step 2 — Combine to 2 good passives
Breed until you get an offspring with the two passives you want together.

### Step 3 — Build to 3 passives
Introduce a second line that adds a third passive, and breed until you get **3 clean passives**.

### Step 4 — Build to 4 passives (optional)
Repeat the process: add one more desired passive from another clean parent line.

This “ladder” approach beats trying to jump straight to 4 passives.

## Practical tips
- Keep “clean line” parents and don’t overwrite them
- Label boxes for each line (e.g., Work Line A, Work Line B)
- Decide your target set first (worker vs combat vs mount)

## Common mistakes
- Using parents with too many random passives
- Trying to get 4 passives immediately
- Not isolating breeding lines (everything becomes mixed and messy)
