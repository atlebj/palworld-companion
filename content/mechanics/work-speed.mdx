# Work Speed Explained

Work Speed is the stat that controls how quickly a Pal completes **work tasks** at your base (crafting, farming, mining, transporting, etc.). If your base feels “slow” even with good Pals, the problem is usually **assignment + pathing + task switching**, not just the raw Work Speed number.

## What Work Speed affects
- Time to complete tasks at workstations (crafting/cooking/production)
- Speed of certain base jobs tied to work suitability (e.g., watering, planting, generating power)
- Overall throughput (items per hour) when the Pal is **actually working**

## What Work Speed does NOT affect
- Capture chance
- Combat damage (unless a passive explicitly says otherwise)
- Player crafting speed (separate mechanic)
- Travel time between jobs (pathing does)

## The real bottlenecks most players miss
### 1) Task switching kills productivity
Multi-skill Pals often bounce between tasks. A “perfect” worker that keeps leaving the station can be worse than a slightly weaker Pal that stays put.

### 2) Pathing and storage placement matter
If a Pal spends half its time walking to storage or getting stuck, Work Speed won’t save you.

### 3) Suitability level still matters
Work Speed helps, but a Pal with better suitability for the job often wins in practice.

## Practical optimization checklist
- Use dedicated workers (avoid generalists for critical production lines)
- Place storage close to the stations that consume/produce items
- Reduce congestion (too many stations in one tight area)
- Use a small number of “transport-only” Pals so crafters don’t leave their stations
- Test changes by timing one craft batch before/after

## Common mistakes
- Stacking workers without fixing pathing
- Letting important crafters also do transport/harvest
- Building huge bases with long walking distances
- Assuming higher Work Speed always beats better suitability
